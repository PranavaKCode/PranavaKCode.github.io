			<h2>About Chess</h2>
			<p>Chess is a two-player strategy board game played on a checkered gameboard with 64 squares arranged in an 8x8 grid. The game has been around for over a thousand years and is still a popular pastime today.</p>
		</section>
		
		<section>
			<h2>Rules of Chess</h2>
			<p>The objective of the game is to checkmate the opponent's king, which means the king is in a position to be captured (in "check") and there is no legal way to remove it from attack. Each player begins with 16 pieces: one king, one queen, two rooks, two knights, two bishops, and eight pawns.</p>
			<p>Moves are made alternating between players, with each player moving one piece per turn. Pieces move according to their own unique rules, with the goal of placing the opponent's king under attack. The game can end in a draw if both players agree, if there are not enough pieces on the board to checkmate, or if there have been a series of moves without any captures or pawn moves.</p>
		</section>
		
		<section>
			<h2>Chess Variants</h2>
			<p>There are many different variations of chess, including:</p>
			<ul>
				<li>Blitz chess, where each player has a set amount of time to make their moves</li>
				<li>960 chess, where the starting positions of the pieces are randomly generated</li>
				<li>Three-check chess, where the first player to put their opponent's king in check three times wins</li>
			</ul>
		</section>
